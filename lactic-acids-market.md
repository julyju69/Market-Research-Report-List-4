<p><p>Lactic acids are organic compounds that can exist in three main forms: L-lactic acid, D-lactic acid, and DL-lactic acid. L-lactic acid is the naturally occurring form found in food and living organisms, while D-lactic acid is the mirror image of L-lactic acid and less common in nature. DL-lactic acid is a combination of both L-lactic acid and D-lactic acid. Each type of lactic acid has different uses and applications in various industries such as food, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics.</p></p>
