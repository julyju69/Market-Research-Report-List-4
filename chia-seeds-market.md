<p><p>Chia seeds are tiny black or white seeds that come from the plant Salvia hispanica, which is related to the mint family. They are native to Mexico and Guatemala and have been a staple in the diets of the ancient Aztecs and Mayans. Chia seeds are considered a superfood due to their high nutritional value, being rich in fiber, protein, omega-3 fatty acids, antioxidants, and various vitamins and minerals. They are known for their ability to absorb water and create a gel-like consistency, making them a popular ingredient in puddings, smoothies, and baked goods.</p><p>The Chia Seeds Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 9.1% during the forecast period. Moreover, the food and beverage industry is incorporating chia seeds into a wide range of products, including energy bars, granola, yogurt, and even drinks, further fueling market growth. Additionally, the popularity of chia seeds in the cosmetic industry for their hydrating and anti-inflammatory properties is contributing to the market expansion.</p></p>
