<p><p>The K-12 software market is divided into two main types: Cloud-Based and On-Premises. Cloud-Based software is hosted on remote servers accessed through the internet, allowing for easy scalability, accessibility, and minimal maintenance costs. On-Premises software is installed and run on the school's local computers or servers, requiring more maintenance and upfront costs, but giving schools more control over their data and customization options. Each type offers distinct advantages and considerations depending on the school's specific needs and resources.</p></p>
