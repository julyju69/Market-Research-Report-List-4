<p><ul><li>Phytosequestration</li><li>Rhizodegradation</li><li>Phytohydraulics</li><li>Phytoextraction</li><li>Phytovolatilization</li><li>Phytodegradation</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Phytoremediation involves using plants to clean up contaminated soil, water, and air. Different types of phytoremediation include phytosequestration, where plants store contaminants in their tissues; rhizodegradation, where plant roots break down contaminants in the soil; phytohydraulics, where plants control water levels to reduce contamination; phytoextraction, where plants absorb contaminants from the soil; phytovolatilization, where plants release contaminants into the air; and phytodegradation, where plants break down contaminants through metabolic processes. These different methods offer flexible and eco-friendly solutions for environmental cleanup.</p></p>
