<p><p>Microcalorimeters are used in various industries for thermal analysis and molecular interaction studies. The market is segmented into three main types: Differential Scanning Calorimetry (DSC), Isothermal Microcalorimetry, and Isothermal Titration Calorimetry (ITC). DSC measures heat flow in a material as a function of temperature, while Isothermal Microcalorimetry is used to study chemical reactions at constant temperature. ITC is primarily used for studying molecular interactions in solutions. Each type of microcalorimeter caters to specific research needs in industries such as pharmaceuticals, biochemistry, and material science.</p></p>
