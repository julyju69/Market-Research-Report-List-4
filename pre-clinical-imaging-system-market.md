<p><p>Pre-clinical imaging systems are divided into two main types: standalone imaging systems and multimodal imaging systems. Standalone imaging systems are designed for specific modalities such as MRI, PET, or CT, providing high-quality images for research purposes. On the other hand, multimodal imaging systems combine two or more modalities into one system, allowing researchers to obtain complementary information from different imaging techniques. Both types of systems play a crucial role in pre-clinical research by facilitating the non-invasive visualization and quantification of biological processes in small animal models.</p></p>
