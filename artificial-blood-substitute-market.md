<p><p>Artificial blood substitutes can be categorized into two main types: Perfluorocarbon-based Oxygen Carriers (PFBOC) and Hemoglobin-based Oxygen Carriers (HBOC). PFBOCs are synthetic compounds that can carry and release oxygen, while HBOCs are derived from purified or genetically modified hemoglobin. PFBOCs are known for their high oxygen-carrying capacity and stability, making them useful in emergency situations or when blood transfusions are not readily available. HBOCs, on the other hand, can enhance oxygen delivery to tissues and organs, reducing the need for blood transfusions.</p></p>
