<p><ul><li>Organic Friction Modifier</li><li>Inorganic Friction Modifier</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Friction modifiers are additives used in lubricants to reduce friction and wear between moving surfaces. Organic friction modifiers are derived from organic compounds such as fatty acids, esters, and amines, while inorganic friction modifiers are made from materials like molybdenum disulfide and graphite. Organic friction modifiers are typically more compatible with oil-based lubricants and offer improved fuel efficiency, while inorganic friction modifiers are commonly used in high-temperature and extreme pressure applications due to their heat resistance and durability.</p></p>
