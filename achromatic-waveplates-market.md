<p><p>Achromatic waveplates are optical devices that manipulate the polarization of light without changing its wavelength. They are commonly categorized into two types based on the phase shift they impart on incoming light: Half-wave waveplates provide a 180-degree phase shift, while quarter-wave waveplates provide a 90-degree phase shift. These waveplates find applications in various fields such as telecommunications, spectroscopy, and microscopy due to their ability to control the polarization state of light with high precision and accuracy.</p></p>
