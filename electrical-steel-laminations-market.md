<p><p>Electrical steel laminations market can be segmented by thickness into two types: less than 0.5 mm and above 0.5 mm. Laminations thinner than 0.5 mm are commonly used in high-efficiency motors and transformers due to their reduced eddy current losses. On the other hand, laminations thicker than 0.5 mm are preferred in applications where mechanical strength is a main concern, such as in power distribution transformers. Both types cater to different requirements in the electrical steel industry, offering specialized solutions for various applications.</p></p>
