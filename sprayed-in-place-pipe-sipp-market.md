<p><p>Sprayed-In-Place Pipe (SIPP) is a trenchless pipe rehabilitation method used to repair and restore deteriorating underground pipes. This technology involves spraying a polymer mortar lining onto the existing pipe, creating a new, smooth inner surface that improves flow capacity and prevents leaks and corrosion. The SIPP market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years, driven by the increasing need for infrastructure rehabilitation in aging cities and the cost-effectiveness and efficiency of SIPP compared to traditional pipe restoration methods.</p><p>The Sprayed-In-Place Pipe (SIPP) Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 9.1% during the forecast period.
