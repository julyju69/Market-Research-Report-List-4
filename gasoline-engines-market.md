<p><p>Gasoline engines are commonly classified into two main types based on their operating cycles: 2-stroke and 4-stroke engines. 2-stroke engines complete a power cycle in just two strokes of the piston, making them lightweight and simple in design, but less fuel efficient and more polluting. On the other hand, 4-stroke engines operate in four strokes of the piston, offering better fuel efficiency and lower emissions, but are heavier and more complex in structure. Both types cater to different market segments based on their specific advantages and disadvantages.</p></p>
