<p><p>The veterinary tomography market includes three main types of imaging systems specifically designed for use in animals. Veterinary MRI systems utilize magnetic resonance imaging technology to produce detailed images of internal anatomical structures. Veterinary CT scanners utilize computed tomography technology to create cross-sectional images of the body. Veterinary SPECT gamma cameras use single-photon emission computed tomography to produce 3D images of tissue function. Each of these imaging modalities plays a crucial role in diagnosing and treating a variety of medical conditions in veterinary medicine.</p></p>
